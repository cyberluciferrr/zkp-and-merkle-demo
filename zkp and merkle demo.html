What the verifier learns
Absolutely nothing about the PIN itself.
The verifier sees: a commitment hash, a random challenge bit, and a consistent response. Every transcript produced in this protocol can be simulated by anyone holding a coin — it reveals zero bits about the actual 4-digit PIN. This is the formal definition of zero-knowledge.
Why the salt matters
Without it, all 10,000 PINs are precomputable.
There are only 10,000 possible 4-digit PINs. Without a salt, an adversary pre-hashes every one and looks up your commitment instantly. The 128-bit random salt makes each commitment unique — making brute force require 2¹²⁸ operations rather than 10,000.
Soundness over 100 rounds
Cheater probability → (½)¹⁰⁰ ≈ 0.
Each round, a cheater must guess the challenge bit with probability 1/2. After 100 independent rounds, the probability of deceiving the verifier every single time is (1/2)¹⁰⁰ ≈ 7.9 × 10⁻³¹ — fewer than the odds of being struck by lightning 100 times in a row.
